Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Smart HR Pro ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website. This policy provides detailed information about what cookies are, how we use them, the types of cookies we use, and how you can control your cookie preferences.

By continuing to use our website, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy. If you do not agree with our use of cookies, you should adjust your browser settings accordingly or refrain from using our website.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies serve various purposes, including:

  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Enabling website functionality and features
  • Analyzing how you use our website
  • Providing relevant content and advertisements
  • Improving your overall user experience

Cookies can be "session cookies" (which are deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (which remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them).

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled in our systems. They are usually set in response to actions you take, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms. Essential cookies enable core functionality such as:

  • Security and authentication
  • Network management and accessibility
  • Maintaining your session
  • Load balancing

Without these cookies, services you have requested cannot be provided. You can set your browser to block or alert you about these cookies, but some parts of the website may not function properly.

3.2 Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. They allow us to:

  • Count visits and traffic sources
  • Measure and improve website performance
  • Understand which pages are most and least popular
  • See how visitors navigate the website
  • Identify and fix technical issues

All information collected by these cookies is aggregated and anonymous. If you do not allow these cookies, we will not know when you visited our website and will not be able to monitor its performance effectively.

3.3 Functionality Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as:

  • Remembering your language preference (English or Arabic)
  • Remembering your theme preference (light or dark mode)
  • Storing your form data to prevent loss during navigation
  • Personalizing content based on your interests
  • Remembering your login details (if applicable)

These cookies may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages. If you do not allow these cookies, some or all of these features may not function properly.

3.4 Targeting and Advertising Cookies

These cookies may be set through our website by our advertising partners. They may be used to:

  • Build a profile of your interests
  • Show you relevant advertisements on other websites
  • Measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns
  • Limit the number of times you see an advertisement

These cookies are based on uniquely identifying your browser and device. If you do not allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising.

3.5 Social Media Cookies

These cookies are set by social media platforms (such as LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and WhatsApp) that we have integrated into our website. They enable you to:

  • Share content with your friends and networks
  • Track your browsing activity across different websites
  • Build a profile of your interests

These cookies may impact the content and messages you see on other websites you visit.

  • Block third-party cookies only

  • Receive notifications when cookies are being set

For Individuals

Can you help me recover unpaid salary from my employer?
Yes, we specialize in salary recovery cases. We can help you file a complaint with the Labor Office, gather necessary documentation, represent you in labor disputes, negotiate with your employer, and pursue legal remedies through the proper channels. We understand the urgency of these situations and work quickly to recover your rightful compensation. Time is critical - contact us immediately if your salary is overdue.
. . . - .
What are my end-of-service benefits and how do I claim them?
End-of-service benefits (gratuity) are calculated based on your length of service and final salary. For the first five years, you receive 15 days' salary per year; for years beyond five, you receive one month's salary per year. We can calculate your exact entitlement, verify your employer's calculation, assist with filing claims if benefits are withheld, and ensure you receive all amounts owed including unused vacation days. The calculation method varies based on whether you resigned or were terminated.

My employer won't release my Iqama. What can I do? .
If your employer is blocking your Iqama transfer or preventing your exit, we can help you take legal action. We'll guide you through filing complaints with the Labor Office, pursuing your rights through Muqeem portal procedures, documenting the violation, and representing you in proceedings to lift the restriction. Saudi labor law protects employees from unjust detention, and we know how to navigate these situations effectively. Act quickly as there are time limits for filing certain complaints.
. . . .
How much do your services cost for individuals?
We offer affordable services for individuals facing employment issues. Initial consultation is free, and our fee structure depends on the complexity of your case. For salary recovery and labor dispute cases, we often work on a success-based fee arrangement where you only pay when we successfully recover your money. We understand that individuals facing employment problems may have financial constraints, so we strive to make our services accessible while providing effective representation.
. . . .
Can you help with contract disputes?
Yes, we handle all types of employment contract disputes including: contract violations, unfair termination, incorrect salary payments, missing allowances or benefits, working hours disputes, leave entitlement issues, and contract interpretation disagreements. We'll review your contract, identify violations, explain your rights under Saudi labor law, and represent you in resolving the dispute through negotiation or official channels. Bring your contract and any relevant documentation for our review.
: . . .
What should I do if my employer is not paying GOSI contributions?
Employers are legally required to register employees with GOSI and make monthly contributions. If your employer is not paying GOSI, you can lose valuable retirement benefits and insurance coverage. We can help you verify your GOSI status, file complaints with GOSI and the Labor Office, ensure your employer makes the required payments and penalties, and protect your future benefits. This is a serious violation that should be addressed immediately - don't wait until you need these benefits.
. . . - .
Is there a time limit for filing labor complaints?
Yes, there are strict time limits for filing labor complaints in Saudi Arabia. Generally, you must file within one year from the date of termination or the date the violation occurred. For salary claims, the time limit may be shorter. This is why it's critical to act quickly if you have an employment dispute. Delaying can result in losing your right to file a complaint and recover your dues. Contact us immediately if you're facing any employment issues - we'll assess your situation and ensure all deadlines are met.
. . . . . - .
What documentation should I keep for potential labor disputes?
Keep copies of: your employment contract, Iqama, passport, salary slips, bank statements showing salary deposits, work permits and visas, any written communications with your employer (emails, WhatsApp messages), warnings or disciplinary notices, attendance records, leave requests and approvals, and performance evaluations. If your employer refuses to provide salary slips, take screenshots of bank deposits. Good documentation significantly strengthens your case. We can review your documents and identify what additional evidence may be needed.
